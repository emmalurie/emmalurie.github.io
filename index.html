I'm an incoming PhD Student at the UC Berkeley School of Information. Previously, I worked as an undergraduate research assistant for the Wellesley College Cred Lab and the MIT Election Data and Science Lab.

Research

In my research, I explore platform algorithms, online misinformation, fact-checking, credibility, and how to build more equitable sociotechnical systems.

Publications:

  • Lurie, Emma" Challenges of Algorithmically Assigning Fact-checks: A Sociotechnical Examination of Google's Reviewed Claims." Undergraduate Thesis. 2019.
  • Lurie, Emma, and Eni Mustafaraj."Opening Up the Black Box: Auditing Google’s Top Stories Algorithm." Proceedings of the International Florida Artificial Intelligence Research Society Conference. Vol. 32. 2019.
  • Lurie, Emma*, Annabel Rothschild*, and Eni Mustafaraj. "How the Interplay of Google and Wikipedia Affects Perceptions of Online News Sources." Computation + Journalism 2019. Miami, FL.
  • Lurie, Emma, and Eni Mustafaraj. Investigating the Effects of Google's Search Engine Result Page in Evaluating the Credibility of Online News Sources." Proceedings of the 10th ACM Conference on Web Science. ACM, 2018.
